The Journey of Indian Painting

For centuries, Indian painting has evolved through shifting cultures, philosophies, and artistic movements. Each painter shaped the next, carrying forward ideas, styles, and ways of seeing the world.

This timeline maps influential Indian painters across history — not as isolated figures, but as a continuous story of thought, expression, and change.

There is a lineage here. And we’re tracing it.

Lineage of Influence

A cross-century dialogue between artists who reimagined Indian visual language, each leaving marks that echo through modern practice.

  1. 1900s

    Raja Ravi Varma

    Blended European realism with Indian myth imagery; changed popular visual culture.
  2. 1920s

    Rabindranath Tagore

    Late-life abstract expression drawings; broke academic realism norms.
  3. 1930s

    Amrita Sher-Gil

    Brought modernism + introspective Indian female gaze; Three Girls.
  4. 1940s

    Jamini Roy

    Simplified folk-inspired forms; Santhal + Kalighat influence.
  5. 1950s

    M.F. Husain

    Founding Progressive Group; horses + Mother India motifs; national modernist icon.
  6. 1950s

    S.H. Raza

    Bindu series — spiritual abstraction rooted in Indian philosophy.
  7. 1950s

    F.N. Souza

    Raw, distorted, anti-establishment figurative work; shocked post-colonial India.
  8. 1960s

    Tyeb Mehta

    Minimalist, powerful Falling Figures — trauma + violence in modern India.
  9. 1970s

    Bhupen Khakhar

    Everyday queer life & middle-class India told in intimate, humorous forms.
  10. 1980s–2000s

    Atul Dodiya

    Photo-paint collage + memory, cinema, and political symbols in contemporary India.
